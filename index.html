Event Description

Public information systems are information systems available for public use. There are public information systems both in the public sector and in the private sector, and both citizens and businesses may be users of such systems. Online railway ticketing system (IRCTC), airlines reservation systems, banking apps, are classic examples in this case. These systems are designed for the general public (across a varied demography), rather than specialists in a particular field or organization.

For an effective functioning of such systems, it is important to understand the various factors contributing to its working. Analysing such systems from various perspectives is helpful in their improvement. This is a multidisciplinary area of study that borrows from human information interaction, anthropology, information technology and communication or interaction design.

This school offers a comprehensive foundation for the developers of public information systems in public and private sectors. With the help of lectures comprising of a theoretical as well as practical knowledge of such systems, supplemented with sessions applying the knowledge, the participants will be able to gain an extensive understanding of such systems and their design and implementation.
